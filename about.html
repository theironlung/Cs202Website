About

I am a student studying Graphic Design at Western Washington University. Through art people can share mutual understanding of ideas. Design’s intrigue for me is its ability to convey meaning and emotion. Language is not a barrier when communicating visually. Powerful design can spark a movement or lay the foundation for a business’s or event’s reputation. I am interested in both computing and art. I feel design brings elements from each field, the versatility of computing and the expressiveness of art. I hope to find a career in design that allows me to combine my passions in computing and art. Good design inspires people, is beautiful, and is useful in any industry. I will make a difference through good design.

I hold Massimo Vignelli’s interpretation of the meaning of ambiguity in design. The great designer believed that ambiguity in design signified a “…plurality of meanings…”, or perspectives to be taken toward an object, opposed to a lack of meaning. While this can be amusing when guessing at a painter’s muse, design often needs no ambiguity. Cultivating a perspective toward an object is one of a designer’s privileges. This interpretation of ambiguity is paired nicely with Mies van der Rohe’s philosophy, “God is in the details”. Small details are often vital components in maintaining a perspective.
